This guide covers Conduit's **subscription billing** — what an MSP organization pays for Conduit and how that subscription is managed.

:::note
This page covers subscription billing (what an MSP pays WYRE for Conduit). **Reseller-channel billing** — how an MSP marks up and bills its own customers — is a separate system; see the [Subtenant Model](/docs/reference/subtenants/#reseller-scoped-pricing).
:::

---

## Pricing

Conduit is **one flat plan — everything included.** There are no feature tiers, no usage credits, and no per-call metering. You pay one predictable price and get the whole platform.

| | Conduit |
|---|---|
| **Base organization fee** | $399 / month |
| **Agent seats** | 2 included; $39 / additional agent seat / month |
| **Human seats** | $39 / seat / month |
| **What's included** | **Everything** — every feature, no gates |

Everything in the platform is included at the flat price:

- **Unlimited vendor connections** — connect as many of the vendor integrations as you need.
- **Unlimited members** and full **team features** (teams, roles, invitations).
- **Role-based access** — per-vendor, per-role scoping of which tools each role can call.
- **Full audit** — a clear record of every tool call, with CSV export.
- **Log shipping** to your SIEM (Loki, Graylog, LogScale).
- **Prompt capture**, **SSO**, and **service clients (M2M)** for automation.

No per-tier feature gating, no monthly credit allocation, and no per-call rate metering — the capabilities above are all on from day one. You are billed the base organization fee plus $39 for each agent seat beyond the two included and each human seat in the organization.

:::note
The flat fee is the **default catalog**. A self-hosted or white-label deployment can override pricing via the `PLAN_CATALOG` environment variable.
:::

## Starting your subscription

There are two ways to start: a free **14-day trial** of the full platform, or activation via an alpha invite code.

1. **14-day trial** — from the billing page at **`/org/billing`**, start your trial. You get the full platform for 14 days at no charge. If at any point you decide it is not for you, do nothing and the trial closes at the end of the 14 days; if you want to keep using Conduit, add a payment method through Stripe Checkout before the trial ends.
2. **Alpha invite code** — an alpha invite code redeemed at org creation (or later via the billing page) activates the subscription immediately, without going through the trial.

Conduit never stores card data — only the Stripe customer ID and subscription state. See [Security § Billing & Payment Security](/docs/reference/security/#billing--payment-security) for how the payment integration is secured.

## Managing a subscription

From **`/org/billing`**, an org owner can open the **Stripe Customer Portal** — Stripe's hosted surface for updating the card on file, viewing invoices, adjusting seats, and cancelling. Conduit creates a portal session and redirects to it.

## When a payment fails — the dunning lifecycle

If a subscription renewal fails (expired card, insufficient funds), the organization moves through a **dunning lifecycle** rather than being cut off immediately. The lifecycle is designed so a billing hiccup does not instantly break an MSP's operations.

The states, in order:

| State | When | What the customer sees |
|-------|------|------------------------|
| **Payment failing** | First failure, within ~24h | A warning banner; full access retained while Stripe retries |
| **Past due** | 24h – 7d after first failure | A more prominent warning; full access still retained |
| **Final warning** | Inside the WYRE grace window, approaching its end | An urgent banner; full access still retained |
| **Suspended** | Grace window expired, or the subscription is canceled | Platform access is gated |

While in any pre-suspension state, the organization keeps full access — the grace window exists precisely so a failed renewal does not interrupt service. Once the grace window expires, the platform is gated at the service layer until payment is recovered.

### The grace window

The grace window defaults to **7 days** and is configurable per deployment via the `WYRE_DUNNING_GRACE_DAYS` environment variable. The first-failure timestamp is persisted, so the lifecycle survives service restarts and resumes from the correct point.

### Recovery

When a failed payment is recovered (the customer updates their card and Stripe successfully charges it), the organization returns to normal. A recently-recovered org briefly shows a "recovered" confirmation, then returns to the standard no-banner state.

---

## Cancellation

Cancelling a subscription through the Stripe Customer Portal ends the subscription at the end of the current billing period. After that, the organization's platform access is suspended — the data is preserved per the dunning model, so the organization can resume by re-subscribing.
