This page walks an MSP from "I just signed up" to "my first customer's first agent
is making real tool calls" — in one ordered pass. It is the **launch-day reading**:
do these steps in order and your tenant is live by the bottom.

Each step has a one-line summary plus the link to the in-depth guide for that
step. If you only have 30 minutes, this page is enough; if you want the depth,
follow the links as you go.

## Before you start

You need:

- An **Auth0-backed login** for the email you'll use as the MSP owner (Conduit
  uses Auth0 for authentication; you'll set the password during sign-in).
- An **alpha invite code** OR the willingness to start a **14-day free trial of
  the full platform** from the billing page after sign-up.
- **API credentials for at least one vendor** you want to connect — the credential
  is whatever the vendor's portal calls an "API key," "personal access token,"
  or "client credentials" pair. The per-vendor walkthroughs (linked below) tell
  you exactly where to get them.
- (If you'll onboard a customer in this same session) **The customer's name and
  the email of their owner** — the person who will administer the customer
  organization once it's set up.

**Time to complete:** ~30–60 minutes the first time. Subsequent customer
onboardings take 5–15 minutes once the MSP-side setup is done.

## Part 1 — Stand up your MSP organization

### 1. Sign up and create your MSP organization

Go to your Conduit endpoint (`https://conduit.wyre.ai` for the hosted gateway)
and click **Sign in**. Conduit redirects you to Auth0; finish the sign-in flow
with the email you'll use as the MSP owner. On first sign-in, Conduit creates
the MSP organization and lands you on the dashboard.

You're now the **owner** of the MSP organization. There's nothing to install —
Conduit is a hosted gateway.

> See [MSP onboarding](/docs/guides/msp-onboarding/) for the discovery questions
> (vendor inventory, customer profile, identity provider) you'll want answered
> before adding teammates.

### 2. Activate your subscription

Visit **Billing** (`/org/billing`). Conduit ships on a single flat plan:

- **$399/month base** per MSP organization
- **+ $39/month per billable seat**
- **The first two service-client (agent) seats are free** with the base; humans
  always bill from seat 1

Two ways to activate:

- **14-day free trial of the full platform.** Click **Start trial**. No payment
  method required to start; you can add one any time before the trial ends to
  convert to a paid subscription.
- **Alpha invite code.** If your Conduit onboarding contact gave you an invite
  code, enter it on the Billing page. The invite code converts your organization
  directly to a paid subscription.

> See [Billing & plans](/docs/guides/billing/) for the rate-change notice
> policy, payment-method updates, and dunning/recovery behavior if a payment
> fails.

### 3. Connect your first vendor (MSP-organization level)

Vendors connected at the **MSP-organization level** are usable by your own
technicians. (Vendors connected for an individual customer come later, in
Part 2.)

1. Open **Connections** (`/org/connections`).
2. Pick the vendor. **API-key vendors** show a credential form; you paste the
   key and click **Connect**. **OAuth vendors** (Xero, QuickBooks, HubSpot,
   Microsoft 365) redirect you to the vendor's consent screen; you authorize
   and the vendor sends you back to Conduit with the token.
3. Conduit encrypts the credential at rest and runs a health check. A **green
   status** on the Connections page means the connection is ready.

Where the per-vendor credential comes from (Atera "Admin → API", HaloPSA
"Configuration → Integrations → HaloPSA API," etc.) is documented in the
per-vendor walkthroughs linked from the [Vendor connections guide](/docs/guides/vendor-connections/).

### 4. Connect an AI client to your MSP organization

Conduit speaks the **Model Context Protocol** — any MCP-capable AI client
connects. Most common are Claude Desktop and Claude Code; the same pattern
works for Cursor, Cline, Continue, and others.

**Claude Desktop** (edit `claude_desktop_config.json`):

```json
{
  "mcpServers": {
    "conduit": {
      "url": "https://conduit.wyre.ai/v1/mcp"
    }
  }
}
```

**Claude Code** (one CLI command):

```bash
claude mcp add conduit https://conduit.wyre.ai/v1/mcp
```

First use opens your browser for Auth0 sign-in. After sign-in, the JWT is cached
locally and refreshed automatically — you sign in once per device.

> See [Connecting an AI client](/docs/guides/connecting-a-client/) for the
> client-by-client setup (including [Microsoft Copilot Studio](/docs/guides/connecting-copilot-studio/))
> and [Supported clients](/docs/reference/supported-clients/) for the full list.

### 5. Verify your MSP-side setup works end-to-end

Before moving to customer onboarding, prove that the MSP-organization-level
setup actually works. From your AI client:

- Make **one test tool call** against the vendor you connected in Step 3
  ("List my active Atera alerts," "Show me my open HaloPSA tickets," "Pull
  this week's RocketCyber incidents," etc.).
- Open the **Audit log** (`/org/audit`). The call you just made should appear
  with the correct user, vendor, tool name, and timestamp.
- Open the **vendor health** view on the Connections page. The vendor you
  connected should show **green**.

If any of those three doesn't land, fix it now before continuing — the
customer-onboarding steps below assume a working MSP-side setup.

> See [Monitoring your tenant](/docs/guides/monitoring/) for the usage
> dashboard, vendor-health detail, and audit-log filters.

## Part 2 — Onboard your first customer

Conduit is built for **MSPs serving their own customers**. Each customer the
MSP onboards is a **separate organization underneath the MSP** — fully isolated
from every other customer, with its own users, vendor connections, and audit
log, but administered by the MSP.

### 6. Create the customer organization

From the dashboard:

1. Open **Customers** (`/org/customers`) and click **+ Add Customer**. The New
   Customer wizard opens.
2. **Step 1 — Customer.** Enter the customer's organization name. Conduit
   generates a URL slug from the name; adjust if you prefer a different one.
   The slug becomes the customer's MCP endpoint URL:
   `conduit.wyre.ai/v1/mcp/<your-slug>/<customer-slug>`.
3. **Step 2 — Admin.** Enter the email of the person who will own the customer
   organization. They'll receive an invitation by email and set their own
   password via the link.
4. **Step 3 — Review.** By default the customer inherits your white-label
   branding (name, logo, colors, email sender). Toggle inheritance off and
   pick an accent if they need their own. Confirm the summary and click
   **Create customer**.

Conduit creates the customer organization, sets you as the **interim owner**
until the invitee accepts, and sends the owner invitation email.

> See [Onboarding a customer](/docs/guides/customer-provisioning/) for the
> per-field detail of the wizard plus what happens immediately after creation.

### 7. Wire up the customer's vendor access (Onboard MCP wizard)

The customer organization is live but **no vendor tools are exposed yet**. To
make any vendor available to the customer's users, run the **Onboard MCP**
wizard per vendor.

From `/org/customers/<id>`, click **+ Onboard MCP**. The wizard has four steps:

1. **Catalog** — pick a vendor from the catalog.
2. **Wire Up** — choose how the customer's users will reach the vendor's API.
   Three patterns:
   - **Bring Your Own Credentials (BYOC)** — each user supplies their own
     API key. The vendor's audit log shows real names; per-user revocation
     is clean. Best for compliance-sensitive customers and per-seat-billed
     vendors.
   - **Shared credentials** — you supply one set of credentials; all the
     customer's users go through it. Fastest setup; the vendor sees one
     identity for every user's actions.
   - **Self-hosted (on-prem gateway)** — the customer runs the vendor's MCP
     server on their own infrastructure, reached through a small WYRE on-prem
     gateway container. Credentials never leave their network. Best for
     strict data-residency requirements. See the
     [on-prem gateway quickstart](/docs/guides/onprem/quickstart/) for the
     deploy steps.
3. **Config** — for BYOC, pick which of the customer's users get a seat. Each
   seated user receives a setup email asking them to add their own API key.
   For Shared, you enter the credentials once.
4. **Allowlist** — choose which of the vendor's tools the customer's users
   are allowed to invoke. Off by default; check the boxes to grant. Allowlists
   can be scoped per department or per role group.

Finishing the wizard creates an audit-log entry and provisions the chosen
wiring pattern for the customer.

> :::caution[Pending: launch wiring-pattern decision]
> Which wiring patterns are **available at launch** is pending Aaron's
> launch-scope decision (BYOC-only vs. Shared vs. Self-hosted at launch).
> Patterns described above reflect the architectural reality of the wizard;
> the at-launch availability subset will be filled in here once that decision
> lands.
> :::

### 8. Invite the customer's first users

The customer's owner (the person you invited in Step 6 above) signs into the
customer organization via the email-link, sets their password, and lands on the
customer dashboard. From there they invite their own team:

- **From the customer's perspective** (the customer admin signs in to
  `conduit.wyre.ai/v1/mcp/<your-slug>/<customer-slug>`): open **Users**
  (`/org/users`) → **+ Invite** → enter the team-member's email and role
  (`admin` or `member`) → send. The teammate receives an email link.

Alternatively, **as the MSP**, you can administer the customer's users on the
customer's behalf via the customer-detail page tabs (`/org/customers/<id>/users`).

> See [Permissions](/docs/reference/permissions/) for the role model and
> what each role can do.

### 9. The customer's first agent session

The customer's users connect their own AI client to the **customer's** MCP
endpoint (note: this URL is different from the MSP's MCP endpoint — it's the
customer-specific one from Step 6):

**Claude Desktop:**

```json
{
  "mcpServers": {
    "conduit": {
      "url": "https://conduit.wyre.ai/v1/mcp/<your-slug>/<customer-slug>"
    }
  }
}
```

**Claude Code:**

```bash
claude mcp add conduit https://conduit.wyre.ai/v1/mcp/<your-slug>/<customer-slug>
```

On first use, the customer's user signs into Auth0 with their own credentials
(set in Step 8). After sign-in, they can ask Claude (or any MCP-capable client)
to perform vendor actions: *"What tickets did Acme submit this week?"* or
*"Run the threshold-monitor script on Acme's primary server,"* — and the call
flows through Conduit's gateway, with the customer's per-tool allowlist (Step 7,
Allowlist) enforced.

### 10. Verify the customer is live

- The customer's user makes one tool call from their AI client.
- The MSP opens the customer's **Audit Log** tab
  (`/org/customers/<id>/audit`) and confirms the call appears with the
  correct user, vendor, tool, and timestamp.
- The customer's **Usage** tab (`/org/customers/<id>/usage`) shows tool-call
  volume against the customer's allowlisted vendors.
- The MSP-organization's billing increments by one billable seat per the
  customer-user who's now active (per the flat-pricing model in Step 2).

If all four land, the customer is **live**.

## Part 3 — Where to go after launch

Once one customer is live, the same pattern repeats per customer:

- **Repeat Steps 6–10** for each subsequent customer. The MSP-side setup
  (Parts 1) only runs once; each new customer is ~10–15 minutes of wizard time.
- **Set up identity provider auto-provisioning (optional)** —
  [SCIM provisioning](/docs/guides/scim/) connects Entra ID / Okta / JumpCloud /
  Google Workspace to Conduit so new MSP team members are provisioned and
  deprovisioned automatically.
- **Apply white-label branding (optional)** — see the
  [white-label setup guide](/docs/guides/white-label-setup/).
- **Watch the dashboards** — the
  [Monitoring guide](/docs/guides/monitoring/) covers the usage dashboard,
  vendor-health alerts, and audit-log filtering.

The full ordered map (MSP → customer → ongoing administration) lives in the
[Launch onboarding](/docs/guides/launch-onboarding/) guide; this walkthrough is
the single-page version of that journey.

## Go-live checklist

For the launch-day eyeball: every box ticked = your MSP and your first customer
are live.

**MSP-organization side:**

- [ ] MSP organization created (Step 1).
- [ ] Subscription active — trial OR paid (Step 2).
- [ ] At least one MSP-org-level vendor connected and green (Step 3).
- [ ] At least one AI client connected to the MSP-org MCP endpoint (Step 4).
- [ ] One MSP-side tool call succeeded and appeared in the audit log (Step 5).

**Customer side (per customer):**

- [ ] Customer organization created (Step 6).
- [ ] At least one vendor onboarded via Onboard-MCP wizard (Step 7).
- [ ] Customer owner invited and accepted (Step 6 + Step 8).
- [ ] At least one customer-side user with role (Step 8).
- [ ] First customer-side AI tool call succeeded and appeared in the
  customer's audit log (Steps 9 + 10).

## Product gaps surfaced during this walkthrough

The dispatch for this walkthrough named two concepts that **do not currently
exist as first-class Conduit terms**. Documenting both here for product-team
visibility:

### "Workspace" — not a Conduit concept

The dispatch named "workspace concept" as a walkthrough step. The substrate-grep
returns ZERO references to a `workspace` abstraction in the Conduit codebase
(the only matches are: "Workspace-wide settings" used generically in
`reseller-general.ts`, and the vendor name "Google Workspace" in `team-scim.ts`).

The canonical Conduit term for what a single MSP or customer occupies is
**organization**. This walkthrough uses **organization** throughout.

If "workspace" was meant to introduce a sub-organization or team abstraction
(e.g., grouping users within a customer organization), that's a product-design
question. Routed to dev via boss for the product-vs-doc determination.

### "Entity mapping basics" — feature not built

The dispatch named "entity mapping basics" as a walkthrough step.
Substrate-grep returns ZERO references to entity-mapping in the Conduit
codebase. The only past mention was in `pricing.astro` PR #28's tier-feature
list (which Aaron subsequently flagged as needing to align with the single
flat-pricing plan — that PR's tier-list is being replaced).

This walkthrough does NOT include a step for entity mapping, because the
feature does not currently exist. Routed to dev via boss to determine:
(a) deferred-capability for post-launch, in which case the walkthrough adds
the step when it ships, OR (b) speculative marketing-copy that should be
removed from any remaining mentions.
