This is the **ordered journey** to get a Managed Service Provider (MSP) live on
Conduit: from signing up to having your first customer's users calling vendor
tools through the gateway. It is the map, not the detail — each step links to
its own guide.

If you just want to see Conduit work end to end as fast as possible, the
[Getting Started](/docs/guides/msp-onboarding/) quickstart is the shortest
path. Use **this** guide when you are taking an MSP all the way to launch and
want the full sequence and a go-live checklist.

## How to use this guide

Work the steps in order. Each step is a short description plus a link to the
guide that walks it in detail. Steps marked **optional** can be skipped for a
minimal launch and added later.

## The path to launch

### 1. Create your MSP organization

Sign up and create your MSP organization. You authenticate through your
identity provider; there is nothing to install. See
[MSP onboarding](/docs/guides/msp-onboarding/) for the build-out detail.

### 2. Activate your subscription

Start a free 14-day trial of the full platform, or activate immediately with an
alpha invite code. Conduit is one flat plan — every feature is included. See
[Billing & plans](/docs/guides/billing/).

### 3. Connect your vendor tools

Connect the vendor tools your MSP uses (RMM, PSA, documentation, security, and
more) so their capabilities are available through Conduit. Most vendors take an
API key; a few use OAuth. See [Vendor connections](/docs/guides/vendor-connections/)
for the catalog, credential scopes, and how to connect each one.

### 4. Connect an AI client

Point an MCP client at your Conduit endpoint and sign in once. Conduit works
with **any MCP-compatible client** — Claude Desktop, Claude Code, Cursor, Cline,
Continue, and more. See [Connecting an AI client](/docs/guides/connecting-a-client/).

### 5. Set up your team and access

Invite your technicians, assign roles, and scope which vendors and tools each
role can reach (deny-by-default is recommended). See
[MSP onboarding](/docs/guides/msp-onboarding/) for team setup and tool
allowlists, and [Permissions](/docs/reference/permissions/) for the role model.

### 6. Auto-provision users with SCIM (optional)

If you manage identity centrally, connect your identity provider (Entra ID,
Okta, JumpCloud, or Google Workspace) to provision users and teams
automatically. See [SCIM provisioning](/docs/guides/scim/).

### 7. Onboard your first customer

Create your first customer organization underneath your MSP, wire up the
customer's vendor access, and invite the customer's owner. See
[Onboarding a customer](/docs/guides/customer-provisioning/).

### 8. Apply your branding (optional)

Present Conduit under your own brand for the surfaces your customers see. See
[White-label setup](/docs/guides/white-label-setup/).

### 9. Verify you are live

Make a test tool call from your AI client against a connected vendor, confirm
real data comes back, and check that the call appears in your audit log. See
[Monitoring your tenant](/docs/guides/monitoring/) for the usage dashboard,
vendor health, and audit log.

## Go-live checklist

- [ ] MSP organization created and subscription active (trial or paid).
- [ ] Required vendor tools connected and validated.
- [ ] At least one AI client connected and signing in.
- [ ] Team invited; roles and tool allowlists set.
- [ ] (optional) SCIM provisioning connected.
- [ ] First customer organization created and its vendor access wired.
- [ ] (optional) Branding applied.
- [ ] A test tool call returns real data and appears in the audit log.
